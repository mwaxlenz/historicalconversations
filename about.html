About the Show

The past has things to say.

Historical Conversations is a podcast and editorial site featuring dramatized interviews with the historical figures of Cape Cod. The figures speak for themselves — in character, in their own idiom, from the world they knew. A consistent host bridges episodes with narration grounded in place and historical context.

The series follows a fixed five-question format. Every figure answers the same five questions — and as the library grows, so does the reward: hear Thoreau's answers, then Marconi's, then a pirate captain's, and you begin to see this peninsula from angles no single era can show you.

How we make this show — AI transparency

The figure voices in this series are AI-generated (synthetic voice technology), performing scripts grounded in historical research. They are dramatized interpretations — not recordings, and not séances. Every episode is built from a sourced research dossier; where the record is uncertain we say so, and where a story comes from folklore rather than documents, we flag it as tradition. The AI is the instrument. The editorial judgment, the research, and the love of this place are human.

Why some voices wait

The four figures of the Indigenous World — Massasoit, Tisquantum, Iyannough, and Metacom — are held until cultural consultation with the Mashpee Wampanoag is complete and the right voice-casting decision is made. Some stories must be told with their communities, not merely about them.

The host

Michael Waxman-Lenz asks the questions. Based in Brewster, he started this series for one reason: the historical markers on Cape Cod kept raising better questions than they answered.
